Privacy Policy

Last Updated: July 2026

At TinyPixel, accessible from our online domains, one of our main priorities is the absolute privacy of our visitors. This Privacy Policy document outlines the precise structures of personal information that are collected, recorded, and utilized by our performance network interfaces.

If you have additional questions or require more explicit information regarding our active Privacy Policy setup, do not hesitate to reach out through our designated administrative contact pathways.

Core Image Processing Philosophy

TinyPixel functions via structured client-side web application technologies. We do not upload, mirror, transmit, or retain your raw images or graphics on external cloud infrastructure. All calculations, resolution modifications, canvas down-sampling steps, and optimization algorithms execute safely inside your web browser environment. Your sensitive images and documents never leave your physical device workspace.

Log File Procedures

TinyPixel follows standard automated system logging parameters. These log documents automatically compile analytical profiles when individuals visit web servers. All standard hosting providers manage these logs as a baseline parameter of infrastructure analytics.

The information safely parsed inside log structures covers incoming Internet Protocol (IP) routing directions, distinct browser configurations (Chrome, Safari, Firefox), Internet Service Provider (ISP) network signatures, dynamic time stamps, referring exit landing channels, and basic total page hit counts. This metadata is strictly decoupled from identifiable real-world personas and is used exclusively to troubleshoot site operation metrics and enhance layout accessibility.

Cookies and Web Tracking Technologies

Like any other functional online utility, TinyPixel handles localized cookies to save structural configurations, visitor platform adjustments, and page preferences. These metrics allow us to serve dynamic updates based on your browser version layout preferences.

Google DoubleClick DART Cookie Compliance

Google is one of our primary third-party automated vendors. It employs specialized structures commonly known as DART cookies to target customized advertisements to our website visitors based on their personal browsing records across the internet.

If you wish to opt out of DART cookie usage, you can adjust your preferences by visiting the official Google ad network and Content Network Privacy Policy pages at the following URL: https://policies.google.com/technologies/ads.

Advertising Partners & Third-Party Network Protections

Third-party ad networks utilize standard configurations like cookies, JavaScript, and Web Beacons inside advertisements appearing on TinyPixel. These signals track the contextual success of their ad campaigns and personalize ad items directly within your browser workspace.

Please note that TinyPixel possesses no access to or supervisory administration over programmatic tracking components managed by external algorithmic third-party ad platforms.

General Data Protection Rights (GDPR & CCPA Statement)

We want to ensure you are fully aware of all your data protection rights. Every user utilizing our optimization dashboard is fully entitled to the following parameters under international frameworks:

  • The Right to Access: You can explicitly request print duplicates of your administrative logs or metadata.
  • The Right to Rectification: You maintain absolute authority to update incomplete or inaccurate data indexes.
  • The Right to Erasure: You can request that our technicians delete accumulated performance metrics under clear terms.
  • The Right to Restrict Data Processing: You can request limits on how your user analytics data is processed under specific circumstances.

Children's Online Information Safeguards

Another essential facet of our security commitment involves providing additional safeguards for children during online activity. We strongly urge parents, legal guardians, and educators to monitor and guide children's internet interaction habits.

TinyPixel does not deliberately compile or index Personally Identifiable Information from children under the age of 13. If you suspect your child has inadvertently provided this information on our platform, contact us immediately so we can promptly remove it from our logs.
